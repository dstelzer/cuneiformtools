This page lets you search the Zeichenlexikon for signs containing a particular component, or whose names match a particular regex. Enter a part of a sign (and/or a regex) in the boxes above, then click "Search".

Tips for effective searching by component:

  • Winkelhaken and downward diagonals often look very similar. If you're not finding anything, try swapping one for the other.
  • Scribes don't always distinguish downward-over-upward and downward-over-horizontal as well as they should. If you can't find your sign with one, try the other.
  • Use () sparingly; if it's not clear that the strokes are meant to cross, it's likely [] or {} instead.
  • The tenû modifier is ignored when searching, so only use it when an entire structure is tilted; if it's only a couple strokes, encode them as downward and upward diagonals.
  • You can use a star * to indicate that something should be in a certain position, but it doesn't matter what.

Tips for effective searching by regex:

  • If there's a particular sign you want, just type in its name.
  • Use all-caps, and use the actual characters Ḫ Š Ṭ Ṣ Ŋ rather than H C T, S, J (sorry, I'm working on this one).
  • Use a dot (.) to match any single letter, and dot-star (.*) to match any number of letters.
