In this experiment, you will be shown a series of cuneiform signs, one at a time. Your goal is to identify each one and enter its name as accurately as possible.

Since it can be difficult to type accents on an unfamiliar computer, you can use the numbers 2 and 3 in place of the acute and grave accents (Á = A2, À = A3), H instead of Ḫ (ḪA = HA), and C instead of Š (CA = ŠA). Note that the difference between Š and S is important for logograms (ŠI and SI are different signs), so C is a better alternative than S.

All of the signs in this experiment are logograms, and none of them are used for V, CV, or VC syllables in Hittite (though they might be in Sumerian or Akkadian).
